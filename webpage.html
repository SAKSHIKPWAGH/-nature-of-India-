India UNESCO Sites:

There are 42 UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India. Santiniketan, established by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore, became the 41st site to be declared a world heritage site in India in September 2023. The UNESCO also included the Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas in Karnataka in the list in September 2023. This takes the total number of UNESCO World Heritage sites in India to 42.

Indian Forests:

Forests, also known as the “green lungs of the earth”, and play a crucial role in sustaining and maintaining the planet's natural equilibrium and balancing the food cycle.
